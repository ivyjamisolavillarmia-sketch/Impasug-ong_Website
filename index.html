01 · About the Municipality

More than highlands — it's a living heritage.

Nestled in the mountain ranges of Bukidnon, Impasug-ong is one of Mindanao's most unspoiled highland municipalities. Its sprawling plateaus, cool climate, and rich Higaonon indigenous culture make it a gem largely undiscovered by the mainstream tourist trail.

Known for its vast ranchlands, pine-covered mountains, ancestral domain of the Higaonon tribe, and the breathtaking Del Monte pineapple plantations, Impasug-ong is not just a place. It is a living, breathing heritage — rooted in the earth, sky, and people.

Where the highlands breathe — every trail, every river, every field of green tells the story of a people who have always lived close to the earth.

Kaamulan Festival

The grandest tribal gathering in Bukidnon, Kaamulan ("to gather as a tribe") celebrates the seven indigenous peoples of the province. The Higaonon of Impasug-ong play a central ceremonial role — performing ancient chants, ritual dances, and presenting authentic tribal regalia that date back generations. It is a festival of identity, pride, and the living pulse of highland culture.

06 · Highland Produce & Flavors

Grown in the clouds, tasted at the table.

Impasug-ong's cool highland climate produces some of the finest agricultural bounty in the Philippines — from world-famous pineapples to temperate vegetables found nowhere else in Mindanao.

Del Monte Pineapple

Naturally sweeter due to highland altitude — these pineapples supply Asia's canned fruit markets and have defined Bukidnon for a century.

🌍 Exported Worldwide

Bukidnon Corn

Grown across the vast rolling plateau, supplying the Philippine poultry and livestock industry. Ground as native grits, it's the taste of Impasug-ong's earth.

🌾 Agricultural Staple

Highland Vegetables

Carrots, cabbage, potatoes — temperate crops that thrive in Impasug-ong's cool mountain air, rarely found elsewhere in Mindanao.

❄️ Cool Climate Grown

Native Highland Coffee

Slow-grown in highland soil by indigenous farming families — robust, aromatic, and entirely of this land.

🍃 Single Origin

Bukidnon Beef & Dairy

The vast communal ranch and private ranchlands have long fed the Philippine cattle industry. Grass-fed, high-quality, highland-raised.

🏞 Ranchland Heritage

Pineapple Jam & Preserves

Homemade pineapple jam crafted from fresh-picked highland fruit — sweet, tangy, and the most beloved pasalubong of Impasug-ong.

🎁 Top Pasalubong

Higaonon Abacá Crafts

Hand-woven abacá bags, baskets, and textiles from the Higaonon community — made using ancient techniques with natural highland fibers.

08 · History & Milestones

A highland story, rooted in time.

From ancestral domain to modern municipality, Impasug-ong's story is written in the land itself — shaped by indigenous heritage, colonial history, and the resilient spirit of its mountain people.

Pre‑1900

Higaonon Ancestral Domain

The highlands are home to the Higaonon and Bukidnon tribes — living in harmony with the forest, following the laws of Magbabaya.

1920s

Del Monte Arrives

Del Monte Philippines begins large-scale pineapple cultivation in the Bukidnon highlands, transforming the agricultural identity of the region.

1948

Impasug-ong Founded

The Municipality of Impasug-ong is officially established, carving its identity from the broader Bukidnon highlands.

1970s

Ranching & Horse Culture

The communal ranch and large cattle estates flourish, embedding the equestrian traditions that define Impasug-ong to this day.

1986

Kaamulan Festival Formalized

The grand Kaamulan Festival is formally celebrated across Bukidnon, with Impasug-ong's Higaonon community playing a central ceremonial role.

Present

Emerging Tourism Destination

Panimahawa Ridge, CEDAR Twin Falls, Mt. Dulang-Dulang, and the Communal Ranch draw adventurers nationwide. Impasug-ong is Mindanao's finest highland escape.

10 · Plan Your Visit

Everything you need to get here.

✈️

Getting Here

Fly into Laguindingan Airport (CDO) in Cagayan de Oro, then take a bus or van headed to Bukidnon. From CDO, Impasug-ong is approximately 2.5 to 3 hours by road through scenic mountain highways.

🏨

Where to Stay

Impasug-ong offers farm stays, nature lodges, and highland resorts with stunning views. Del Monte's Highland Estates also offers guided agri-tourism accommodations.

🍲

Local Eats

Try pochero Bukidnon style, native corn grits, and the freshest highland vegetables. Don't miss the native pineapple juice — sweet and chilled by the mountain air.

🚌

Getting Around

Habal-habal (motorcycle taxis) and multi-cabs serve the municipality. For remote barangays, a 4×4 vehicle is recommended.
